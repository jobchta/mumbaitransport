Mumbai Metro Network

Line 1 - Blue Line

Route: Versova ↔ Ghatkopar via Andheri (Elevated, 11.4 km, 12 stations)

Status: Operational (Inaugurated: 8 June 2014)

Features: Cut travel time from 71 to 21 minutes; connects Western and Central suburban rail lines.

Lines 2A & 2B - Yellow Line

Line 2A (Dahisar East ↔ D N Nagar): ~18.6 km, 17 stations, opened April & January 2022–23

Line 2B (D N Nagar ↔ Mandale): ~23.6 km, 20 stations, under construction (expected December 2025–27)

Line 3 - Aqua Line (Colaba–Bandra–Aarey)

Status: Partially operational (~22 km open since Oct 2024), full length 33.5 km expected by August 2025

Stations: 27 (26 underground, 1 at-grade)

Note: New segment saw 65,000+ riders in first weekend.

Lines 4, 4A, 10, 11 - Green Line

Line 4: Wadala ↔ Kasarvadavali (~32.3 km)

Line 4A: Kasarvadavali ↔ Gaimukh (~2.7 km)

Line 10: Gaimukh ↔ Shivaji Chowk (Mira Road) (under environmental review)

Line 11: Wadala ↔ CSMT (proposed)

Lines 5 & 12 - Orange Line

Line 5: Thane ↔ Bhiwandi ↔ Kalyan (~24.9 km), under construction

Line 12: Kalyan ↔ Taloja (proposed)

Line 6 - Pink Line

Route: Lokhandwala ↔ Vikhroli (~14.5 km), 13 stations, elevated

Interchanges: Yellow Line (Adarsh Nagar), Aqua Line (SEEPZ), Green Line (Kanjurmarg West), Red Line (Jogeshwari-Vikhroli), Suburban railway (Western & Central lines)

Expected opening: By December 2025

Lines 7, 7A & 9 - Red Line

Line 7: Dahisar East ↔ Andheri East (~16.5 km), operational since April 2022

Line 7A: Extension to CSMIA, includes tunnels, tunneling completed July 2025

Line 9: Dahisar East ↔ Mira Bhayandar (under construction)

Line 8 - Gold Line

Proposed route: CSIA ↔ Navi Mumbai International Airport (~35 km)

Line 13 - Purple Line

Proposed route: Shivaji Chowk (Mira Road) ↔ Virar (~23 km)

Line 14 - Magenta Line

Proposed route: Vikhroli–Kanjurmarg–Badlapur (~45 km)
